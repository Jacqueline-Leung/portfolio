What is HTML semantic elements?

What is HTML semantic elements? From w3school.com, it says “Semantic elements = elements with a meaning”. These elements self-explain clearly what they are, their tags express what kind of content they contain, which help clearly send the messages to both the browser and the developer, in turn facilitate the coding and understanding process. “div” and “span” can be examples of non-semantic elements, which tell us close to nothing; while “form” and “table” can be examples of semantic elements, which describe clearly what they are. According to the W3C: "A semantic Web allows data to be shared and reused across applications, enterprises, and communities." As the semantic elements clearly describe the meaning of the elements themselves, they simplify interpretation and lessen confusion, help other computers and programmers to understand the content and structure of a website, make internationalization and interoperability possible.

About the recipe

The recipe & Bethany Lutheran Church

It is from Bethany Lutheran Church Centennial Cookbook, p.189.

To celebrate the 100th anniversary of Bethany Lutheran Church, the church and WELCA complied this cookbook. It is a collection of favorite recipes contributed by the church members.

Let's gather the ingredeients and get started!

Here are the ingredients:

  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon soda
  • 2 tablespoons sugar

Here are the steps:

  1. Preheat Æbleskiver pan over medium-low heat.
  2. Beat yolks; add sugar, salt, and milk.
  3. Sift together flour, soda and baking powder. Add to wet ingredients.
  4. Beat egg whites until stiff. Fold into batter.
  5. Bake in butter or shortening in pan until sides are set and golden brown, about 3 minutes.
  6. Using wooden skewer, carefully turn Æbleskiver over.
  7. Bake until bottom is golden brown, about 2 minutes. Transfer to platter.
  8. A slice of apple can be added to each Æbleskiver before turning.

Serving ideas: Sprinkle generously with powdered sugar and serve with raspberry jam.

What is Æbleskiver?

Æbleskiver (Danish pronunciation: [ˈeːpləˌskiːwɐ], [singular: æbleskive]) are Danish snacks that are spherical in shape. The name literally means apple slices in Danish, although apples are not usually an ingredient in present-day versions. The crust is similar in texture to European pancakes, but with a light and fluffy innard like a popover. The English language spelling is usually aebleskiver or ebleskiver.(Wikipedia)
